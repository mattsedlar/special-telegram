The healthcare sector is one of the most important sources of jobs in the economy. Healthcare spending reached $3.2 trillion in 2015 or 17.8 percent of GDP (Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services 2016) and accounted for 12.8 percent of private sector jobs (Bureau of Labor Statistics 2017). It was the only industry that consistently added jobs during the Great Recession. Overall private sector healthcare employment grew by 20 percent between 2005 and 2015. In 2016, this industry, which is the focus of this report, added 381,000 private sector jobs, the most of any industry (Bureau of Labor Statistics 2017). It is a particularly important source of employment for workers without a college degree, most of whom, as we document in this report, earn low wages.
